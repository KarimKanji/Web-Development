FAQ

Is there an anabolic window after workouts?

No, there are many modern studies that suggest that there is not
a window after a workout where you build muscle "extra" well...
However, consuming lots of carbohydrates after a workout helps
to replenish glycogen stores in the muscles.

Does more calories equal to more muscle?

Short anwser is no. In the fitness industry there is alot of
misconception when it comes to calories. Yes, the body needs
energy to make muscle but the amount needed is much less than
people are led to believe. A surplus of 100-300 calories a day
with 300 being an absolute maximum is more than enough to
maximise muslce gain. Remember muscle is gained slowly.

How much muslce should i expect to gain?

The average person should expect to gain between 3-6kg of lean
muscle per year and will expect to get deminishing returns after
the third year of active proper training. If you gain more you are
most likley mistaking your weight gain for fat, glycogen and water
not muscle. Remember for every gram of carbohydrate 3 grams of water
is stored as glycogen in all muscles!

I want to be lean and build muscle, is it possible?

Yes it is possible to build muscle in a calorie deficit as long as
its not to steep of a deficit and as long as you get sufficient
protein. Remember protein is the building block for new muslce.
If you are in a calorie deficit but have sufficient protein intake
your body will burn fat for energy. Usually the safest way to gain
muscle is to "maingain", which basically means that you don't "bulk"
but you eat enough protein and you either burn fat or maintain
your current fat %.

How much rest should i have between sets?

Studies have shown that for smaller muscle groups like the biceps
sufficient ATP (Adenosine triphosphate) is replenished in 1min 30sec - 2min
but remember this is for heavy all-out sets. This means training
harder than last time and not leaving anything in the tank.
For more compound movements with sets of extreme intensity stuides have
shown that a rest period of 2min 30sec - 4min is appropriate for maximum
strenght in the following set. If you are a beginner you should certainly
be on the lower end of the spectrum because the likleyhood of your sets
being very intense is low.
